Abstract

RubbishLM is an end-to-end writing system for archive-ready AI papers. It combines outline planning, related-work stitching, reviewer-aligned phrasing, result framing, and page-level consistency checks. The page is designed as the flagship AI sample in the ICMLpaper 2025 archive.

Keywords: LLM, automated writing, archive readiness, consistency checking, reviewer simulation

1. Introduction

This sample paper argues that the hardest part of paper automation is not local fluency but page-level consistency. Titles, abstracts, figures, tables, and metadata must point to the same story.

2. Related Work

Prior work on academic writing, reviewer modeling, and long-form generation informs the design, but the main contribution here is a page-level archive pipeline rather than a single writing tool.

3. Methodology

The method integrates outline generation, related-work assembly, reviewer alignment, result framing, and consistency checking. The goal is not to predict real conference outcomes, but to produce pages that look complete and internally coherent.

5. Limitations

The current system is strongest in the AI track and still relies on templates for domain transfer. It cannot guarantee scientific novelty or reproducibility.

6. Ethics Statement

This page is satirical and should not be used to justify academic misconduct. The system is for page organization and parody only.

7. Conclusion

As the flagship AI sample, RubbishLM shows how an archive-focused writing system can improve coherence across sections, figures, and metadata.
